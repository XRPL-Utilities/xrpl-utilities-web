Active Float

3-second settlement supply

Total Circulating Supply tells you how much XRP is out of escrow. Modeled Active Float tells you how much XRP can actually absorb an institutional block trade inside a 3-5 second XRPL ledger close. Custodial omnibus balances at exchanges are mostly user inventory, not orderbook depth, so we apply a 10% active-depth proxy ratio (industry estimates run 5-15%) to the live exchange omnibus and add AMM-locked XRP plus on-chain DEX depth.

Why these two numbers differ

Subtractive models (total minus tagged custody minus dormant) miss the point: institutional cold custody is intentionally untagged on-chain, so anything not subtracted gets implicitly classified as liquid. Additive inverts that: only count XRP that is verifiably resting on an open market right now. When UPbit holds 6.5B XRP across labeled wallets, those are omnibus accounts backing user balances, not orderbook depth. The 10% proxy ratio converts custodial inventory into an estimate of what is actually on the book.

Each component is surfaced in the paid /scan response under derived_models.active_float.mathematical_bridge so an agent can recompute under its own ratio.

Reading the variables

M · Modeled Active Float. The XRP that can actually absorb an institutional block trade inside a 3-5 second ledger close. Additive: exchange omnibus × 10% proxy ratio, plus AMM-locked, plus DEX orderbook depth.

V · how many times each unit of M is reused for settlement in a year. There is no canonical XRPL velocity, which is why this is a slider. Empirically low today (most XRP is held, not transacted); a settlement-network future would push it higher.

Q · total dollar value of settlement you assume XRPL clears in the same year. Q and V must use the same time window, so we lock both to annual.

P · the equilibrium USD-per-XRP price the identity requires given M, V, and Q. The calculator solves for this. Not a forecast.

What it means

Why P comes out small: today's liquid XRP supply, at the assumed velocity, can clear the assumed annual settlement using only a tiny fraction of XRP's current market value as utility. Pure settlement-floor math does not justify today's spot price.

The gap between P and the market is everything else XRP is priced for: bets on future adoption, fixed supply (XRP cap is 100B), and store-of-value behavior. None of that is in this math, by design. Burst Math is settlement-only; it is a floor, not a forecast.

What would push P up

  • Q - Rises · more real annual settlement on XRPL. Plausible drivers: Ripple's ODL adds new cross-border corridors, treasuries adopt XRPL for B2B settlement, agent-payable x402 flows scale, RLUSD volume grows.
  • M - Shrinks · less XRP resting on open markets. Plausible drivers: institutions sweep exchange omnibus into long-term cold custody, AMM TVL grows, market makers pull resting orderbook depth as settlement volume builds.

Reading the controls

P₀ · the implied floor today. Copied live from Burst Math. Change Q or V there to re-anchor everything below.

Monthly supply constriction · how fast liquid M shrinks per month. 1%/mo means 1% of circulating XRP exits each month into vaults, escrow, AMM TVL, or dormancy.

Monthly volume growth · how fast annual settlement Q grows per month. 3%/mo compounds to roughly 1.4× volume by month 24.

±15% band · a visual reminder that the baseline is a point estimate and reality is noisier. It is not a statistical confidence interval.

What it means

Small monthly rates compound aggressively. 1%/mo constriction is 21% of supply gone in 24 months. 3%/mo volume growth is roughly 1.4× volume by month 24. Both push P up.

Watch where the baseline crosses the dashed spot line. That is the math saying: under these specific assumptions, the implied utility floor catches up to today's market price somewhere in the next 24 months. It does not mean spot stays still in reality; it means the speculation and adoption premium fully materializes as settlement utility on this trajectory.

Sensitivity, not forecast. Regulatory regime, macro liquidity, AMM depth, and exchange behavior all matter, and none of them compound smoothly. Treat the chart as "what would the math say if these specific rates held" rather than a price target.

For developers & AI agents

Pay $0.10 per snapshot, no API key

XR-Telemetry implements x402 v2 with the exact scheme on XRPL mainnet. Verification and settlement are delegated to the t54 XRPL facilitator.

  1. 1. POST /quote returns an invoice with amount in drops, recipient, deep link, and QR code.
  2. 2. Pay the XRPL Payment from any wallet. Poll GET /status/{invoice_id} until paid: true.
  3. 3. GET /results/{invoice_id} returns the full TelemetryPayload: supply, liquidity, AMM, utility floor.
Swagger UI → agents.json (raw) →
# pip install x402-xrpl requests
import requests

quote = requests.post(
    "https://telemetry.xrpl-utilities.io/quote",
    json={},
).json()

# Sign & submit XRPL Payment from your wallet using quote["deep_link"]
# Then poll until paid:
while not requests.get(
    f"https://telemetry.xrpl-utilities.io/status/{quote['invoice_id']}"
).json()["paid"]:
    time.sleep(5)

payload = requests.get(
    f"https://telemetry.xrpl-utilities.io/results/{quote['invoice_id']}"
).json()

print(payload["supply"])
print(payload["utility_floor"])
