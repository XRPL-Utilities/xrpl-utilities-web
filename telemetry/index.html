Burst Math

Implied price floor calculator

This is the macroeconomic Equation of Exchange (M · V = P · Q) applied to XRPL over a one-year window. Money supply times velocity equals price times throughput. We anchor M (today's liquid XRP) from the snapshot, you choose Q and V for the year, and the math solves for P: the lowest USD-per-XRP price that lets the supply absorb the assumed settlement demand. The market trades higher than P because XRP is not priced only on settlement utility; speculation, scarcity bets, and network-adoption expectations all add premium on top.

Reading the variables

M · the liquid XRP supply that can actually move. Total XRP minus dormant minus escrowed. Anchored from the live snapshot.

V · how many times each liquid XRP is reused for settlement in a year. There is no canonical XRPL velocity, which is why this is a slider. Empirically it is low today (most XRP is held, not transacted); a settlement-network future would push it higher.

Q · total dollar value of settlement you assume XRPL clears in the same year. Q and V must use the same time window, so we lock both to annual.

P · the price each XRP would need to be worth for M and V to clear Q over that year. The calculator solves for this.

What it means

Why P comes out small: today's liquid XRP supply, at the assumed velocity, can clear the assumed annual settlement using only a tiny fraction of XRP's current market value as utility. Pure settlement-floor math does not justify today's spot price.

The gap between P and the market is everything else XRP is priced for: bets on future adoption, fixed supply (XRP cap is 100B), and store-of-value behavior. None of that is in this math, by design. Burst Math is settlement-only; it is a floor, not a forecast.

What would push P up

  • Q - Rises · more real annual settlement on XRPL. Plausible drivers: Ripple's ODL adds new cross-border corridors, treasuries adopt XRPL for B2B settlement, agent-payable x402 flows scale, RLUSD volume grows.
  • M - Shrinks · less XRP actually circulating. Plausible drivers: Ripple's monthly 1B unlock continues to be mostly relocked, the SingleAssetVault amendment activates and locks supply away, AMM TVL grows, more long-term holders go dormant.

Reading the controls

P₀ · the implied floor today. Copied live from Burst Math. Change Q or V there to re-anchor everything below.

Monthly supply constriction · how fast liquid M shrinks per month. 1%/mo means 1% of circulating XRP exits each month into vaults, escrow, AMM TVL, or dormancy.

Monthly volume growth · how fast annual settlement Q grows per month. 3%/mo compounds to roughly 1.4× volume by month 24.

±15% band · a visual reminder that the baseline is a point estimate and reality is noisier. It is not a statistical confidence interval.

What it means

Small monthly rates compound aggressively. 1%/mo constriction is 21% of supply gone in 24 months. 3%/mo volume growth is roughly 1.4× volume by month 24. Both push P up.

Watch where the baseline crosses the dashed spot line. That is the math saying: under these specific assumptions, the implied utility floor catches up to today's market price somewhere in the next 24 months. It does not mean spot stays still in reality; it means the speculation and adoption premium fully materializes as settlement utility on this trajectory.

Sensitivity, not forecast. Regulatory regime, macro liquidity, AMM depth, and exchange behavior all matter, and none of them compound smoothly. Treat the chart as "what would the math say if these specific rates held" rather than a price target.

  1. 1. POST /quote returns an invoice with amount in drops, recipient, deep link, and QR code.

  3. 3. GET /results/{invoice_id} returns the full TelemetryPayload: supply, liquidity, AMM, utility floor.
