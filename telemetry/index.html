Active Float

3-second settlement supply

Total Circulating Supply is how much XRP exists outside Ripple's escrow program. Modeled Active Float is how much of that supply is actually available to absorb a settlement burst inside a single 3-5 second XRPL ledger close. The two differ because most XRP held at exchanges sits in customer accounts, not posted on the order book, so we estimate the slice that is actually quoted and add the XRP committed to AMM pools and the XRP resting on the on-chain DEX.

Why these numbers differ

Most XRP held at exchanges is sitting in customer accounts, not posted on the order book. When an exchange wallet holds 6.5B XRP, the vast majority of that is user balances backing withdrawals and trades, not depth a market sweep can actually hit inside a few seconds. Counting it as "available" overstates how much can really clear in a settlement burst.

We don't count the full exchange holdings. We apply a 10% multiplier as an estimate of how much is genuinely on the book at any moment. Only a small slice of any exchange's holdings is actively quoted at one time. We then add the XRP committed to XRPL AMM pools, which is always swappable inside a single ledger close, plus the XRP resting in the on-chain DEX order book.

The paid /scan response includes every input number and the multiplier so anyone receiving the data can recompute under their own assumptions.

Burst Math

Required equilibrium price calculator

The Equation of Exchange (M · V = P · Q) says the supply available for trade (M) times how often each unit changes hands per year (V, velocity) equals the price per unit (P) times the dollar volume of transactions per year (Q). Plug in three values and you can solve for the fourth.

M is anchored at Modeled Active Float, the supply that can clear inside a single ledger close. Q is your assumption about how much annual settlement volume XRPL will see. V is your assumption about how often that supply turns over per year. With those three set, the math returns P: the per-XRP price the identity requires under those specific assumptions. Not a forecast. Real prices come from what the next seller is willing to accept, not from an identity. Read P as "what the math requires."

Reading the variables

M · Modeled Active Float. The XRP that can actually clear inside a single 3-5 second ledger close. Computed as 10% of exchange-held XRP, plus all XRP in AMM pools, plus XRP resting in the on-chain DEX order book.

V · how many times each unit of M is reused for settlement in a year. There is no canonical XRPL velocity, so this is your assumption. The default of 6 is a midpoint estimate for a XRPL where ODL-style cross-border flows recycle the same liquid supply multiple times a year. Today's real velocity is much lower (most XRP is held, not transacted); 6 reflects what a mature settlement network looks like, not what XRPL is right now.

Q · total dollar value of settlement you assume XRPL clears in a year. Q and V must use the same time window, so both are annual.

P · the per-XRP USD price the identity requires given M, V, and Q. The calculator solves for this. Not a forecast.

What it means

P typically comes out far below the market price. That is the point. Today's liquid XRP supply, at the assumed velocity, can clear the assumed annual settlement using only a tiny fraction of XRP's current market value as utility. Pure settlement-floor math does not justify today's spot price.

The gap between P and the market is everything else XRP is priced for: bets on future adoption, the fixed 100B supply cap, and store-of-value behavior. None of that is in this math, by design. Burst Math is settlement-only. It is a floor, not a forecast.

What would push P up

  • Q - Rises · more real annual settlement on XRPL. Plausible drivers: Ripple's ODL adds new cross-border corridors, treasuries adopt XRPL for B2B settlement, agent-payable x402 flows scale, RLUSD volume grows.
  • M - Shrinks · less XRP resting on open markets. Plausible drivers: institutions move XRP from exchanges into long-term cold custody, AMM pool sizes grow, market makers pull back resting orderbook depth as settlement volume builds.

Reading the controls

Starting price · the implied floor today. Copied live from Burst Math. Change Q or V there to re-anchor everything below.

Monthly supply constriction · how fast liquid M shrinks each month. A setting of 1% per month means 1% of circulating XRP exits each month into AMM pools, cold custody, escrow, or dormancy.

Monthly volume growth · how fast annual settlement Q grows each month. A setting of 3% per month compounds to roughly 1.4 times the starting volume by month 24.

±15% band · a visual reminder that the baseline is a point estimate and reality is noisier. It is not a statistical confidence interval.

What it means

Small monthly rates compound aggressively. 1% per month of supply constriction works out to about 21% of supply gone in 24 months. 3% per month of volume growth works out to roughly 1.4 times the starting volume by month 24. Both push P up.

Watch where the projected baseline crosses the grey dashed line representing today's market price. That crossover is the math saying: under these specific assumptions, the implied utility floor catches up to where the market trades today, somewhere in the next 24 months. It does not mean spot stays still in reality. It means the speculation and adoption premium fully materializes as settlement utility on this trajectory.

Sensitivity, not forecast. Regulatory regime, macro liquidity, AMM depth, and exchange behavior all matter, and none of them compound smoothly. Treat the chart as "what would the math say if these specific rates held" rather than a price target.

For developers & AI agents

Pay $0.10 per snapshot, no API key

XR-Telemetry implements x402 v2 with the exact scheme on XRPL mainnet. Verification and settlement are delegated to the t54 XRPL facilitator.

  1. 1. POST /quote returns an invoice with amount in drops, recipient, deep link, and QR code.
  2. 2. Pay the XRPL Payment from any wallet. Poll GET /status/{invoice_id} until paid: true.
  3. 3. GET /results/{invoice_id} returns the full TelemetryPayload: supply, liquidity, AMM, utility floor.
Swagger UI → agents.json (raw) →
# pip install x402-xrpl requests
import requests

quote = requests.post(
    "https://telemetry.xrpl-utilities.io/quote",
    json={},
).json()

# Sign & submit XRPL Payment from your wallet using quote["deep_link"]
# Then poll until paid:
while not requests.get(
    f"https://telemetry.xrpl-utilities.io/status/{quote['invoice_id']}"
).json()["paid"]:
    time.sleep(5)

payload = requests.get(
    f"https://telemetry.xrpl-utilities.io/results/{quote['invoice_id']}"
).json()

print(payload["supply"])
print(payload["utility_floor"])

Output shape

  • supply: total / circulating / escrowed / dormant XRP, AMM-locked, exchange omnibus + venue/address counts, DEX orderbook depth, HODL wave %, 24h exchange outflow, escrow release÷relock ratio

  • generated_at: ISO timestamp the snapshot was computed
